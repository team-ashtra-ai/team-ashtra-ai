# Client Discovery Questionnaire Template

Use this as a reusable consultation framework for website, brand, systems, SEO, UX, and delivery projects.

The best ash-tra consultation should not feel like a giant wall of text. Use this as a guided conversation:

- begin with the business problem and what is not working
- use short forced-choice prompts when the client feels unsure
- bring in examples, references, screenshots, and visual comparisons early
- move from business clarity to audience, brand, website scope, and then constraints
- turn the answers into a concise brief that can shape the preview and the final build direction

## Recommended Consultation Flow

1. Understand the business
2. Understand the audience
3. Define brand direction
4. Define website and system requirements
5. Confirm constraints, priorities, and risks

## 1. Core Business Understanding

### Business basics

- What does your business do?
- What do you sell or provide?
- Who do you help?
- What problem do you solve?
- How do clients currently find you?
- What makes your business different from competitors?
- What stage is your business at right now: idea, new, growing, established, rebranding?
- What services or products are the most important?
- Which services/products are the most profitable?
- Which services/products do you want to grow most?

### Business goals

- Why do you want this project now?
- What are you hoping this project will achieve?
- What would success look like in 3 months?
- What would success look like in 12 months?
- Is your priority more leads, sales, credibility, bookings, awareness, automation, or something else?
- What are your biggest business challenges right now?
- What is currently not working?
- What would make you say this project was worth the investment?
- Are there any urgent problems this project must fix?
- If we only solved one thing for you, what should it be?

### Decision-making

- Who is involved in making decisions?
- Who has final approval?
- Will multiple people give feedback?
- Who will maintain the project after launch?
- How quickly can you usually review and approve things?
- Has anything gone wrong in past projects because of too many decision-makers?
- Are there any internal politics or sensitivities we should know?
- What concerns do you have about starting this project?
- What has stopped you from moving forward before?
- What would make this process easier for you?

## 2. Business Analyst Discovery

### Business model and operations

- How does a customer go from first contact to final purchase?
- What does your current sales process look like?
- What happens after a client becomes a customer?
- What parts of your work are manual and time-consuming?
- Where do delays usually happen?
- What tasks do you repeat every day or week?
- What systems do you currently use?
- Are you using spreadsheets, CRM, email tools, WhatsApp, booking tools, invoices, or paper processes?
- What information do you need to collect from customers?
- What internal processes need to be improved?

### Requirements gathering

- What must this system/site/platform absolutely do?
- What would be nice to have but not essential?
- What would you like to automate?
- What actions should a user be able to complete on the website?
- What data do you need to capture?
- What reports or visibility do you need?
- What permissions or user roles are needed?
- Do different types of customers need different journeys?
- Do staff members need dashboards or admin areas?
- What business rules must the system follow?

### Pain points and inefficiencies

- Where do leads get lost?
- What causes confusion for clients?
- Where do you waste time?
- What mistakes happen repeatedly?
- What do clients ask you over and over?
- What information is hard to track?
- What is difficult to manage at scale?
- What tasks depend too much on one person?
- What part of the process feels disorganized?
- What part of the business feels harder than it should be?

### Priorities and scope

- What is most important to build first?
- What can wait until phase two?
- Which features are business-critical?
- Are there seasonal deadlines or launch dates?
- What must be live at minimum?
- What would you cut if budget became tight?
- What must not be cut?
- Are there legal/compliance requirements?
- Are there operational bottlenecks we must solve before launch?
- Do you want a simple MVP first or a full system?

## 3. Marketing Strategy Discovery

### Market position

- How do you describe your business in one sentence?
- What do you want people to think or feel when they hear your brand name?
- Why should someone choose you instead of competitors?
- What promise are you making to customers?
- What makes your offer valuable?
- Are you seen as premium, affordable, specialist, modern, friendly, exclusive, or something else?
- What do you want to be known for?
- What do you not want to be known for?
- Have you defined your positioning before?
- Are there brands whose positioning you admire?

### Audience and customer personas

- Who is your ideal client?
- Who usually buys from you in real life?
- Are your ideal client and actual client the same?
- What age range are they typically in?
- What life stage are they in?
- What industry or profession are they in?
- What are their goals?
- What are their fears, frustrations, and objections?
- What problem are they actively trying to solve?
- What words would they type into Google when looking for your service?

### Customer behavior

- How do customers usually discover you?
- What questions do they ask before buying?
- What stops them from buying?
- What reassures them?
- What do they compare you against?
- How long is the decision-making process?
- Is the purchase emotional, practical, expensive, urgent, or highly considered?
- Do customers need education before they convert?
- Do they trust websites, referrals, social proof, experts, or low prices most?
- What content would help them move forward?

### Offer and messaging

- What exactly are you offering?
- How do you currently describe your services?
- Which messages have worked well before?
- Which messages have not worked?
- What language does your audience use?
- What tone should the brand have?
- Do you want to sound formal, warm, luxurious, expert, approachable, bold, calm, or disruptive?
- What objections need to be addressed on the website?
- What proof points do you have: testimonials, numbers, awards, case studies, credentials?
- What call to action do you want people to take?

### Marketing channels

- Which channels are you currently using?
- Which channels bring the best results?
- Do you want help with SEO, email, paid ads, organic social, partnerships, or referrals?
- Are you willing to produce regular content?
- Do you have an email list?
- Do you run ads?
- Are you active on social media?
- Which platforms matter most?
- Are there channels you do not want to use?
- What does your ideal lead generation system look like?

## 4. Brand / Visual Identity Designer Discovery

### Brand personality

- If your brand were a person, how would you describe it?
- What 3 to 5 adjectives should define the brand?
- What should the brand feel like?
- What emotions should customers experience?
- What do you want to avoid visually?
- Should the brand feel more classic or modern?
- Should it feel minimal or expressive?
- Should it feel premium or accessible?
- Should it feel corporate or human?
- Should it feel serious or playful?

### Visual preferences

- Do you already have brand colours?
- Do you want to keep them or change them?
- Are there colours you strongly like?
- Are there colours you dislike or want to avoid?
- Do you want a bright, muted, dark, light, luxurious, earthy, soft, or bold palette?
- Do you prefer clean and minimal or rich and detailed?
- Do you like strong contrast or subtle elegance?
- Do you prefer lots of white space or more visual density?
- Should the brand use photography, illustration, icons, textures, or abstract shapes?
- Do you prefer modern sans-serif fonts, elegant serif fonts, or something more distinctive?

### Reference-based questions

- Which brands or websites do you find beautiful?
- Which brands feel aligned with your taste?
- Which designs feel too cold for you?
- Which designs feel too childish?
- Which ones feel too cheap?
- Which ones feel too complicated?
- What do you specifically like in those examples: layout, colour, typography, imagery, simplicity?
- What do you specifically dislike?
- Are there competitor brands you want to stand apart from visually?
- Are there industries whose style you admire even if unrelated?

### Existing assets

- Do you already have a logo?
- Do you have brand guidelines?
- Do you have fonts, templates, icons, illustrations, or photography?
- Do you have high-quality brand images?
- Are your current assets consistent?
- Do you need a full rebrand or just refinement?
- Are there files available in editable formats?
- Are there legal constraints around logo or imagery use?
- Is your branding currently working anywhere well?
- What existing asset absolutely must stay?

## 5. Website Strategist / Website Designer Discovery

### Website purpose

- Why do you need a website?
- What is the main purpose of the site?
- Is it meant to inform, generate leads, sell, book calls, build trust, or support clients?
- What is the number one action a visitor should take?
- What would make the site successful?
- Is the site replacing an old one or starting from zero?
- What frustrates you about your current site?
- What do users need to find quickly?
- What should the site communicate within the first 5 seconds?
- What should the site make easier for your team?

### Site structure

- What pages do you think you need?
- Which pages are definitely required?
- Which pages are optional?
- Do you need a homepage, about, services, pricing, FAQ, contact, blog, case studies, booking, portal, resources?
- Do you need landing pages for campaigns?
- Do you need pages for different audiences or services?
- Should content be available in multiple languages?
- Do you need gated content or downloadable resources?
- Do you need legal and policy pages?
- Do you need account or member-only areas?

### Content and user journey

- What should a new visitor understand immediately?
- What information must appear above the fold?
- What common questions should the site answer?
- What order should users discover information in?
- What trust signals should be visible?
- What action should a visitor take on each main page?
- Where do users currently get confused?
- Which content already exists?
- Who will provide text, images, and media?
- Do you need help writing the website copy?

### Design preferences

- Do you want the site to feel modern, elegant, bold, editorial, premium, minimal, corporate, soft, or friendly?
- Are there websites you love?
- What do you like about them?
- Are there websites you dislike?
- What do you dislike about them?
- Do you prefer large imagery or text-led design?
- Do you want animation? If so, subtle or expressive?
- Do you want the site to feel highly unique or familiar and easy?
- How important is mobile design?
- Are accessibility features important for your users?

## 6. UX / UI Designer Discovery

### User goals and flows

- What are the main tasks users need to complete?
- What does a successful user journey look like?
- What might confuse a first-time visitor?
- What information do users need before taking action?
- Are there multiple types of users?
- What should each type of user see first?
- What is the simplest path from arrival to conversion?
- Where do users usually drop off?
- What questions do users ask before proceeding?
- Do users need guidance, comparison tools, filters, forms, steps, or dashboards?

### Forms and friction

- What forms do users need to complete?
- What information is essential versus optional?
- How long can forms realistically be?
- What might make users abandon a form?
- Do users need file upload?
- Do forms need save-and-return later?
- Do users need progress indicators?
- Do you need conditional logic in forms?
- What confirmation should users receive after submitting?
- What support options should be available during the process?

### Accessibility and clarity

- Are there users with language, technology, age, or accessibility barriers?
- Should content be especially simple or beginner-friendly?
- Do you need larger text, contrast options, keyboard access, subtitles, or reduced motion?
- Are users likely to be on mobile more than desktop?
- Are users often multitasking or in a hurry?
- Do users need multilingual support?
- Are there any compliance expectations for accessibility?
- What would make the experience feel easy and trustworthy?
- What would make it feel stressful or confusing?
- What do you want users to never struggle with?

## 7. Developer / Technical Lead Discovery

### Technical requirements

- Do you already have a website or system?
- What platform is it on?
- Do you want to keep the current platform or rebuild?
- Do you need a CMS?
- Who will update the content after launch?
- Do you need the site to be easy for non-technical staff to edit?
- Do you need integrations with other systems?
- Do you need user accounts or admin access?
- Will there be payments, bookings, forms, or automation?
- Do you expect high traffic or future scaling?

### Integrations

- Do you use a CRM?
- Do you use email marketing tools?
- Do you need calendars, scheduling, payment gateways, chat, WhatsApp, invoicing, document upload, analytics, ad tracking, or automation tools connected?
- Do you use Google Workspace, Microsoft, HubSpot, Salesforce, Mailchimp, Stripe, Calendly, Zapier, Formspree, Airtable, or something else?
- What data needs to sync between systems?
- What currently requires copy-pasting between tools?
- Do you need notifications or workflows triggered automatically?
- Do you need API integrations?
- Do you need multilingual or multicountry support?
- Are there external systems we must not break?

### Security and compliance

- Will users submit sensitive information?
- Do you store client documents?
- Do you need secure uploads?
- Are there privacy laws to follow?
- Do you need consent checkboxes or cookie controls?
- Do you need backups and recovery?
- Who should have access to what?
- Do you need audit trails?
- Is there any regulated data involved?
- Are there legal disclaimers or jurisdiction issues?

### Hosting and maintenance

- Do you already own the domain?
- Where is the site hosted?
- Who controls the hosting, DNS, and domain?
- Do you need staging and production environments?
- Do you need support after launch?
- Do you need documentation or training?
- Do you need performance optimization?
- Do you need uptime monitoring?
- Do you want to avoid ongoing high costs?
- What is your tolerance for technical complexity?

## 8. SEO / Content Strategist Discovery

### SEO goals

- Do you want more traffic from Google?
- Are there specific services or topics you want to rank for?
- What locations matter?
- What languages matter?
- Are you targeting local, national, or international users?
- Which pages should bring in the most traffic?
- Are you trying to rank for informational, commercial, or transactional searches?
- Have you done SEO before?
- Do you know which keywords currently bring traffic?
- Do competitors outrank you for important terms?

### Content inventory

- Do you already have website copy?
- Do you have blog articles, guides, FAQs, case studies, videos, downloads?
- Is your content current or outdated?
- Who writes content now?
- Do you need content strategy, copywriting, or editing?
- What topics do customers ask about most?
- What questions should content answer clearly?
- Do you want thought leadership content?
- Do you want evergreen SEO pages?
- Do you want content reused across social, email, and website?

### Messaging and tone

- What tone should the content have?
- Should it be formal, educational, persuasive, expert, friendly, authoritative, or conversational?
- Are there words or claims you want to avoid?
- Do you need compliance review on content?
- What terms do your customers naturally use?
- What makes your business credible in writing?
- What proof should be built into the copy?
- Should content feel concise or comprehensive?
- Should the site sound more like a professional firm, a modern startup, a trusted guide, or a premium boutique?
- What do you want readers to do after reading?

## 9. Sales / CRM / Operations Discovery

### Lead handling

- What happens when someone fills out a form?
- Who follows up with leads?
- How quickly do you respond?
- Do you qualify leads before speaking to them?
- What information do you need before a consultation or sale?
- What makes a lead high quality?
- What makes a lead not a fit?
- Do you need auto-replies?
- Do you need lead scoring?
- Do you need reminders, tasks, or pipelines?

### Client management

- What information do you need to keep on file?
- What stage labels do you use for clients?
- Do you need document collection?
- Do you need portals or status tracking?
- Do you need payment status visibility?
- Do you need appointment history?
- Do you need internal notes or case records?
- Do multiple staff members need access?
- Do you need email templates?
- What would make internal management easier?

## 10. Analytics / Performance Discovery

- What metrics matter most to you?
- How do you currently measure success?
- Do you track leads, sales, calls, bookings, downloads, traffic, or conversion rates?
- Which actions should count as conversions?
- Do you need Google Analytics, Tag Manager, Meta Pixel, LinkedIn Insight, or ad tracking?
- Do you run campaigns that need landing page measurement?
- What reports do you want to see regularly?
- Who needs access to performance dashboards?
- Are there KPIs tied to business decisions?
- What numbers would tell you the project is working?

## 11. Project Manager Discovery

### Timeline

- When do you want this live?
- Is there a hard deadline?
- Why that deadline?
- What happens if launch is delayed?
- Are there seasonal or campaign-related timing pressures?
- Are you available for weekly feedback?
- Will content or approvals cause delays?
- Are there upcoming events tied to the launch?
- What can be phased later if needed?
- Is quality or speed more important?

### Budget

- What budget range do you have in mind?
- Are you looking for a simple version first or the full vision now?
- What matters more: speed, custom design, scale, or low cost?
- Are you comfortable paying for tools/subscriptions?
- Do you prefer lower setup cost or lower long-term cost?
- Is there room for future phases?
- What would make the investment feel justified?
- What would make the project feel too expensive?
- Are there hidden internal costs we should consider?
- What is your priority if trade-offs are needed?

## Special Questions for Clients Who “Have No Idea What They Want”

### A. Forced-choice questions

- Do you want the brand to feel more luxury or friendly?
- More modern or classic?
- More minimal or rich?
- More serious or energetic?
- More expert-led or community-led?
- More soft and elegant or bold and powerful?
- More corporate or human?
- More premium or accessible?
- More clean and calm or expressive and memorable?
- More traditional trust or innovative edge?

### B. Taste discovery

- Show me 3 websites you like. What exactly do you like about each?
- Show me 3 websites you dislike. What feels wrong?
- Which feels more like you: Apple, Nike, Chanel, IKEA, Airbnb, Deloitte, or none of these?
- If your brand were a hotel, what type would it be?
- If your brand were an outfit, what would it look like?
- If your brand were a room interior, what would it feel like?
- What brands do you naturally trust?
- What visual style makes you feel uncomfortable?
- What design feels too cheap for your business?
- What design feels too formal for your audience?

### C. Audience empathy questions

- What is your customer worried about before contacting you?
- What do they secretly want most?
- What do they not understand yet?
- What would make them trust you immediately?
- What would make them leave the website?
- What words would make them feel understood?
- What do they need explained simply?
- What objections do they have in their head?
- What result are they hoping for?
- What kind of support do they expect?

### D. Practical simplifiers

- What kind of clients do you enjoy working with most?
- What kind of clients do you not want?
- What kind of work do you want more of?
- What kind of work do you want less of?
- Which competitor looks better than you right now?
- Which competitor sounds better than you?
- What part of your current presence embarrasses you?
- What part are you proud of?
- If your site had to convince someone in 30 seconds, what should it say?
- If we had to start with one simple version, what would it include?

## Questions About Brand Voice

- How should the brand sound?
- Should the tone be warm, polished, elegant, direct, expert, reassuring, inspiring, calm, technical, or simple?
- Should the copy use plain language or more specialist language?
- Should the brand sound more premium or more approachable?
- Are there words or phrases you always use?
- Are there words you never want used?
- Should the tone be more personal or more institutional?
- Should the site sound conversational or formal?
- Should it educate first or sell first?
- Should the writing feel short and punchy or rich and detailed?

## Questions About Content Supply

- Do you already have content we can use?
- Do you have brochures, presentations, old website copy, WhatsApp messages, email templates, or PDFs?
- Do you have testimonials?
- Do you have case studies?
- Do you have professional photos?
- Do you need stock images?
- Who will provide missing content?
- Do you want us to interview you and turn that into copy?
- Can you record voice notes instead of writing?
- Do you need a content collection checklist?

## Questions About Competitors

- Who are your main competitors?
- Which competitors do you respect?
- Which competitors do you dislike?
- What do competitors do better than you?
- What do you do better than them?
- What do competitors’ websites communicate well?
- What do they communicate badly?
- How do you want to stand apart?
- Are you competing on quality, trust, speed, price, specialization, convenience, or design?
- What market gap do you believe exists?

## Risks and Constraints

- What are you worried this project might get wrong?
- What has disappointed you in past agencies or freelancers?
- What mistakes must we avoid?
- Are there technical limitations already in place?
- Are there legal, brand, or internal approval constraints?
- Are there tools you must keep using?
- Are there tools you refuse to use?
- Are there budget constraints that affect scope?
- Are there security concerns?
- Is there anything sensitive or confidential we need to plan around?

## Final Alignment Questions

- In one sentence, what do you most want this project to achieve?
- What does “good enough” look like?
- What does “excellent” look like?
- What are your top 3 priorities?
- What are your top 3 worries?
- What must be included in phase one?
- What can be delayed until later?
- What do you want the client experience to feel like?
- What kind of impression should people leave with?
- Is there anything important we have not asked?

## Interviewing Note

Clients who say they “have no idea” usually still have strong preferences. Use simple, concrete prompts instead of abstract marketing language.

Ask:

- Who do you most want to attract?
- Do you want the business to feel more premium or more approachable?
- Which websites feel right to you?
- What kind of customer do you enjoy working with?
- What should people feel when they land on your website?
