# Client Discovery Questionnaire Template

Use this as the internal consultation framework for premium redesign and rebuild projects.

The public site should stay elegant and short.
This document is for deeper conversations once a lead is qualified.

## Core Principles

- begin with the business problem
- use direct language
- bring examples and references in early
- move from business clarity into audience, brand, website, and constraints
- turn the answers into a concise working brief

## 1. Business

- What does the business do?
- What does it sell or provide?
- Who is it for?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why is a new site needed now?
- What currently feels weak, outdated, or underwhelming?
- What would make the project feel worthwhile?

## 2. Audience

- Who is the ideal client?
- What kind of buyer is the site trying to attract?
- What do they care about?
- What do they fear?
- What makes them trust a brand quickly?
- What would make them leave the site?

## 3. Brand Direction

- What should the brand feel like?
- What should it definitely not feel like?
- Should it feel more premium, more modern, more cinematic, more minimal, or more expressive?
- What brands or sites feel aligned?
- What brands or sites feel wrong?

## 4. Website Goals

- What is the main job of the site?
- What should users understand within five seconds?
- What is the number one action a visitor should take?
- Which pages are essential?
- What kind of proof can be shown publicly?

## 5. Visual Preferences

- Do you want the site to be more image-led or more text-led?
- How important is motion?
- What kind of typography feels right?
- What kind of color palette feels right?
- What kind of visual treatment feels too cheap or too generic?

## 6. Technical Expectations

- Does the site need a CMS?
- Does the site need multilingual support later?
- Are there any integrations required now?
- What should never happen technically?
- How important are mobile performance and accessibility?

## 7. Content

- What content already exists?
- What proof exists?
- What content is missing?
- Who will provide text, images, and references?
- Does the site need help with copy direction?

## 8. Sales And Fit

- What makes a lead high quality?
- What makes a lead not a fit?
- What information is needed before a consultation?
- What kind of buyer does the business want more of?
- What kind of buyer does it want less of?

## 9. Success Criteria

- What should success look like in 3 months?
- What should success look like in 12 months?
- What would “good enough” look like?
- What would “excellent” look like?
- What are the top three priorities?
- What are the top three worries?
