FAQ

Questions that usually come up before a premium rebuild.

Short answers about fit, motion, mobile quality, search structure, private work, and how the process usually starts.

What does ASH-TRA actually do?

ASH-TRA redesigns and rebuilds websites for brands that want a sharper, more premium, more modern technology-grade digital presence.

Can the site feel more premium without turning into visual chaos?

Yes. That is the goal. Stronger atmosphere and motion should still support clarity, not bury it.

Will it still work well on mobile?

It has to. Mobile-first quality is part of the concept from the start, not a cleanup task after desktop is finished.

Can the site include animation and richer visuals?

Yes. Motion, scenes, image-led sections, and animated surfaces are all possible as long as they stay controlled and performant.

Will the site still be SEO-friendly?

Yes. SEO is handled as structure: metadata, semantics, hierarchy, readable content flow, and technical hygiene.

Do you share client portfolios publicly?

Not by default. Public examples stay as direction studies. Approved client work can be discussed privately when useful.

Who is the work best suited for?

Ambitious founders, consultants, agencies, AI brands, and modern service businesses that care about design quality and digital perception.

What kinds of projects are not a fit?

If the goal is the fastest, cheapest, most generic template possible, this is probably not the right fit.

Do I need a fully formed brief before reaching out?

No. A short explanation of what feels weak now and what you want the new site to feel like is enough to start.

How do quotes usually work?

Quotes are shaped around scope, level of polish, strategic depth, and whether the work is a redesign or a fuller rebuild.

Still not sure?

The easiest way to get clarity is to describe the current site honestly and ask whether the gap sounds like a fit.
